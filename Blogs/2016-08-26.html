Rotation 3

Hosted By Jaydon Webb

We carried on with the Wine Rotation system for our third wine club, each bringing a different wine type. We met at Jaydon's house where his parents spoiled us with delicious offerings for our pairings. His Dad had made divine little sirloin steaks, there were chicken skewers, small quiches, bread with spreads and cheesy nachos among others. We even rounded off with chocolate mousse for dessert!

Back to the wines though... This month, we tasted three white wines, a rosé and a red wine. First up was the Klauwer Cellars Chenin Blanc 2016, a light tropical wine to get us started. This was followed by the Durbanville Hills Chardonnay 2015, a much stronger, dry wine that paired quite well with Thai Chicken. Our third white wine was the Bellingham Wines Pear Tree White 2015, a blend of Chenin Blanc and Viognier, which paired easily and was a balanced, sweet wine. Next up, was the Orange River Cellars Rosé 2015 which many of us felt tasted like berries and candy. We rounded off the tastings with the Diemersfontein Pinotage 2014, a dark red wine with intense flavours of chocolate and coffee. It was not everyone's style, though it did pair amazingly well with the sirloin steaks!!

The winning wine for the evening was the Bellingham Wines Pear Tree White blend of Chenin Blanc and Viognier. It was a delicious and easy drinking wine that paired very well with different foods.
