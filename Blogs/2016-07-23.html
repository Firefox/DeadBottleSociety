Rotation 2

Hosted By Bronwen Smith

After our first successful wine club, we decided to carry on with the wine type rotation so that we would have a good range of different wines. We set out a feast of pairings to go with our five wines of the evening and the tasting got under way.

We started out with the Nederberg Lyric 2015, a white wine blend of Savignon Blanc, Chenin Blanc and Chardonnay, a pleasant start that we found paired well with curry and spicy food. Next up was the first of two Rosé wines for the evening, the Two Oceans Shiraz Rosé 2015 followed by the Robertson Winery Natural Sweet Rosé. Both were highly enjoyable and easy drinking. We ended off the evening with two red wines. The first was the Alexander Fontein Cabernet Savignon 2014, a very heavy wine that we could imagine would suit a more seasoned palette. The last wine for the evening was the Graham Beck Railroad Red, Red blend 2013. We found this wine to be very strong, bitter and heavy. It also did not pair well with any of the pairings on offer. It definitely was not a favourite though it did lead to some very amusing commentary amongst the group.

The top wine for the night was definitely the Two Oceans Shiraz Rosé, followed by the Robertson Natural Sweet Rosé in a close second.
