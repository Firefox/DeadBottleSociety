Rotation 4

Hosted By Matt Renaud

The fourth wine club brought about the last round of the wine rotation set up. From next month, we will be trying out different themes for each evening. We spent some time brainstorming different ideas so we will see how that goes!

Matt hosted this evenings wine club, and set out the table with lots of different, tasty food offerings. As usual, everyone arrived with pairings to suit their wines, and before long we were faced with a feast. The wine selection for the evening included three white wines and two red wines.

We started off with a charity wine, the Amy Savignon Blanc 2015. Amy Biehl was a student who was sadly killed in South Africa in 1993. Her parents set up the Amy Biehl Foundation, a non-profit organisation helping youth in impoverished communities to have a brighter future. This wine honours her memory and a portion of the sales are donated to the Foundation. We found the wine to be quite strong and crisp though it paired well with spicy food. Our second white wine was the Laibach Vineyards The Ladybird Chenin Blanc 2014. We could pick up that the wine had been in a barrel as the taste was woody. Overall, it was a smooth and drinkable wine. The third white wine was the Overhex Wines Balance 2016, a white wine blend of Sauvignon Blanc and Semillon. The wine was smooth with very subtle, almost watery flavours, though enjoyable overall.

The next wine was the Oakridge Ruby Cabernet Cinsault 2015, a smooth wine with a delicate hint of chocolate that can be found on the palette. It was surprisingly easy drinking and enjoyable for a red wine! It came out tops as the winning wine for the evening. Our final wine was the Stonedale Shiraz 2015, a very strong and bitter wine. It was definitely our least favourite tasting of the night!
